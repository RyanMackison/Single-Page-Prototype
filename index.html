About Penguins

Penguins are flightless, semi-aquatic, sea birds that live almost entirely in the southern hemisphere. They are highly adapted to the cold ocean water by having plumage, or a layer of feathers, to keep them warm, and using flippers to swim fast. Most penguin species eat smaller sea life, which they catch with a spiny tongue and powerful jaws to catch prey while swimming.


When people think of penguins, they don't think of the different species of penguins. That raises the question: how many species of penguins are there? Scientists and experts recognize 18 different species of penguins. Some of the penguin species include: the emperor penguin, the king penguin, the little penguin, and the royal penguin. You can learn more about the different species of penguins on the species page!
